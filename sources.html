CO₂ Emissions per Kilometer: Data Sources

Overview

The CO₂ emissions figures used in our Travel Environmental Footprint Calculator are based on average estimates from reputable environmental and transportation agencies. These values provide a general understanding of the carbon impact associated with different modes of transportation.

Sources of Emission Factors

  • Car:

    Average emissions of 0.192 kg CO₂/km are sourced from the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA). This value represents a typical gasoline-powered passenger car.

  • Bus:

    Average emissions of 0.089 kg CO₂/km per passenger are derived from the International Energy Agency (IEA) research on public transportation emissions.

  • Train:

    Average emissions of 0.041 kg CO₂/km per passenger are based on data from the European Environment Agency (EEA).

  • Flight (Short-haul):

    Average emissions of 0.254 kg CO₂/km per passenger are taken from the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) reports on aviation emissions.

Considerations

Please note that these emission factors are average estimates and actual emissions can vary based on specific conditions such as vehicle type, fuel efficiency, occupancy rates, and regional energy sources. For more accurate assessments, users may need to adjust these values according to their specific circumstances.
